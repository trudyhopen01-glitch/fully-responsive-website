03 — Projects

Work & Prototypes

🫀
ArduinoSensorsBiomedical

Patient Monitoring Concept

A real-time vital signs prototype that reads temperature and heart rate via Arduino-interfaced sensors — exploring the fundamentals of embedded biomedical sensing.

This project investigates how low-cost microcontrollers can handle continuous patient monitoring. Sensor data is processed on-chip and output to a serial monitor, forming a foundation for future IoT-connected clinical devices. The experience deepened my understanding of analog signal conditioning and interrupt-driven programming.
⚙️
ArduinoControl SystemsMechatronics

Closed-Loop Control Prototype

An automated sensor-actuator feedback system demonstrating closed-loop control principles — the core concept behind every robot, servo, and autonomous machine.

Built using Arduino, this prototype reads an environmental input via sensor and actuates a physical response automatically — no manual intervention required. It grounded my understanding of PID logic, feedback loops, and system stability, all central to mechatronics and robotics engineering.
🦾
In ProgressRoboticsWearable

Rehabilitation Assist Device

A conceptual wearable mechatronic device designed to support joint rehabilitation — combining real-time biomedical sensing with actuated mechanical assistance.

Currently in ideation and early CAD design. The device will monitor joint range-of-motion and provide calibrated resistance or assistance during physiotherapy. Mechanical design is being developed in Fusion 360, with Arduino handling control logic. This project connects my biomedical and mechatronics tracks in a single, human-centred application.
